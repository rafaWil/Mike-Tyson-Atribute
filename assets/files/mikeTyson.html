About
Birth and Childhood

Born in 1966 in Brooklyn, New York, Tyson had a tough childhood marked by poverty and crime.

Discovery in Boxing

At age 13, he was discovered by Cus D'Amato, who became his mentor and trained him for professional boxing.

World Champion

In 1986, Tyson became the youngest heavyweight champion, known for his aggressiveness in the ring.

Personal Issues

His turbulent personal life, including a troubled marriage and a 1992 rape conviction, affected his career.

Return and Controversies

After leaving prison, he returned to boxing but faced controversies, including the infamous ear-biting incident with Holyfield in 1997.

Decline and Retirement

With declining performance, Tyson retired in 2005 after a series of losses.

Reinvention

After retirement, he struggled with addiction and financial issues but reinvented himself in various fields, including entertainment.

Current Legacy

Today, Tyson is a respected figure with a successful podcast and is an example of overcoming adversity and resilience.

Records
Youngest Heavyweight Champion

Mike Tyson became the youngest heavyweight champion in boxing history on November 22, 1986, at the age of 20 years and 4 months by defeating Trevor Berbick.

First to Unify Heavyweight Titles

Tyson was the first boxer to unify the three major heavyweight titles (WBC, WBA, and IBF) in 1987, solidifying his status as one of the greatest champions in boxing history.
